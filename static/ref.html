DRIM

DevRel Impact Model

01. The Problem

Developer Relations teams often drown in "opportunity." Every conference, every integration, every blog post feels urgent.

But not all work is equal. Without a clear decision-making framework, resources are allocated based on vibes, internal politics, or whoever shouts the loudest. This leads to burnout, lack of focus, and low-impact outcomes.

"DevRel is not about vibes. It is about resource allocation."

02. What is DRIM?

DRIM (DevRel Impact Model) is a minimalist framework designed to quantify the qualitative. It provides a consistent method to score potential activities so you can ruthlessly prioritize.

Score = (Demand × Reach × Impact) / Maintenance

04. Guidelines

Priority Threshold Scores above 50 indicate immediate priority. These are typically high impact tasks with manageable maintenance costs.
The Maintenance Trap Be cautious of scores between 20-40. These projects often look valuable but can silently drain resources if Maintenance > 6.
Deprecation Zone If Impact is low (< 3) and Maintenance is high (> 7), the activity should likely be killed or automated, regardless of the score.
* Disclaimer: The DRIM model is a heuristic tool for decision support. It is not a replacement for strategic human judgment or company mandates.
